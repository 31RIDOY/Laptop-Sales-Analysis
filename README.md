## Project Insights & Business Recommendations

#### Brand Premium Analysis
  Observation: Brands like Apple, Microsoft, Asus, Razer have the highest average price points, often exceeding %1,500.
  
  Insight: These Brands successfully target the premium and profesional segments by offering specialized hardware and high brand equity.
  
  Recommendation: Retailers should focus marketing efforts for these brands on high-income professionals and creative industries where budget is less of a concern than performance and prestige.


#### The Power of RAM on Pricing
  Observation: There is a sharp price increase when moving from 8GB to 16GB RAM.
  
  Insight: RAM is perceived by customers as the most critical feature, allowing manufactureres to charge a significant premium for highrt memory.
  
  Recommendation: To maintain steady cash flow, the business must ensure a diverse and competitively priced stock of Notebooks, as they are the "bread and butter" of the retail market.

#### The Portability Premium (Ultrabooks)

   Observation: Ultrabooks are consistently priced higher than standard Notebooks, even with similar internal specifications.

  Insight: Customers are willing to pay a "Portability Tax." Lightweight design and sleek aesthetics are high-value features for executives and travelers.

  Recommendation: Marketing campaigns should emphasize "Weight" and "Battery Life" for Ultrabooks, as these are the primary emotional drivers for this customer segment.

#### Operating System Strategy

  Observation: Laptops with No OS or Linux have the lowest prices, while Windows dominates in variety.

  Insight: Budget-conscious buyers and tech enthusiasts (who prefer custom installations) are the primary target for No-OS laptops.

  Recommendation: Promote "No-OS" laptops to students or IT professionals as a way to get better hardware at a lower price, potentially increasing sales in the budget segment.
